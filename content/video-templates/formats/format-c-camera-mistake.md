# Format C — Camera mistake

**Best for:** camera-setup tutorials, camera-and-analysis-education organic Reels, pre-upload education that reduces support load.
**Default length:** 20s–30s.

## Structure

1. Wrong setup — show the common mistake (too close, wrong angle, normal video speed, handheld)
2. Consequence — show what that mistake does to the analysis (blurry motion, cut-off body, unusable frame)
3. Corrected setup — show the right setup side by side or as a hard cut
4. Exact recording requirements — on screen, matching the product's real camera-setup guidance
5. Upload CTA — "Start your Pitch Nav analysis."

## The exact requirements to state (must match `src/app/camera-setup/page.tsx`)

- True side view, from the pitcher's throwing-arm side
- Full body visible in frame
- Camera approximately 15 feet from the pitcher
- Phone lens approximately 6 feet high, level
- Landscape orientation
- Stationary camera — tripod or fixed surface, no handheld
- No digital zoom
- Strong, even lighting
- SLO-MO recording mode only, not normal video slowed down afterward
- 240 FPS preferred, 120 FPS accepted

## Rules specific to this format

- Do not exaggerate the consequence (no "your analysis will fail" — say plainly that a bad angle or normal speed makes it hard or impossible to see what matters).
- Use the real numbers above — do not round or approximate them differently video to video.
- This format doubles as pre-support education — treat accuracy here as a product-support responsibility, not just marketing.

## Footage needed

- A deliberately-wrong demo clip (too close, wrong angle, or normal speed) — can be staged, but must be labeled as a setup demonstration, not a real pitcher's analysis
- A correctly-set-up demo clip from 15 feet, 6 feet high, side view
- Optional: `public/pitch-nav-slow-motion-guide.png` as a screen insert showing the iPhone Settings → Camera → Record Slo-mo path
