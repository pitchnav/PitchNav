# Format A — Mechanics breakdown

**Best for:** organic Reels, mechanics-breakdown content, Collab Reels with a coach.
**Default length:** 20s (see `content/video-templates/timelines/20s.md`); 30s if a drill needs its own beat.

## Structure

1. Strong hook — mechanics-mystery or velocity-curiosity hook, on screen in the first 2 seconds
2. Full-speed pitch — the real rep, no slow motion yet, so the viewer sees "normal" first
3. Freeze frame — stop on the exact phase where the observation is visible
4. One highlighted observation — a single blue overlay or joint-marker animation, one idea only
5. Why it may matter — one sentence connecting the observation to efficiency, consistency, or repeatability (never a guaranteed outcome)
6. One coaching cue or drill — a specific, actionable cue or a single drill rep
7. Pitch Nav product glimpse — dashboard or report frame showing this exact kind of observation gets flagged automatically
8. CTA — "Start your Pitch Nav analysis."

## Rules specific to this format

- Show the full-speed rep before any slow motion — the contrast is the hook's payoff.
- One observation per video. If there are two worth mentioning, that's two videos.
- The coaching cue must be something a viewer could try today, not vague encouragement.
- Never say "this will fix your velocity" — say what the movement pattern is and why it's worth working on.
- Label any measurement or estimate as an estimate, matching the product's own language (see `content/editing-style-rules.md`).

## Footage needed

- Real full-speed side-view pitch, 240 FPS preferred / 120 FPS accepted
- Same rep in slow motion for the freeze-frame beat
- Screen recording of the relevant dashboard/report section
- Optional: drill B-roll if the coaching cue includes a demonstration

## Caption rhythm example

```
THIS LOOKS NORMAL
UNTIL FOOT STRIKE
UPPER HALF OPENS EARLY
WORTH ONE DRILL

START YOUR ANALYSIS
```
