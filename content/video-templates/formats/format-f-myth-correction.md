# Format F — Myth correction

**Best for:** velocity-curiosity and mechanics-education organic Reels, top-of-funnel awareness content.
**Default length:** 20s.

## Structure

1. Common belief — state a widely-held pitching belief plainly, without mocking it
2. Contradiction — the surprising part: why it's incomplete or wrong in practice
3. Visual proof — real footage that demonstrates the contradiction (not just a voice-over claim)
4. Simple explanation — one sentence of the actual mechanism at play
5. Takeaway — what to do with this information
6. CTA — "Start your Pitch Nav analysis."

## Rules specific to this format

- Correct a belief; don't invent a strawman nobody holds. The myth must be one real pitchers/parents/coaches actually repeat.
- The "contradiction" must be demonstrated on real footage, not just asserted in voice-over or text.
- Keep the explanation grounded in mechanics and sequencing — never framed as a guaranteed velocity claim or a medical/injury claim.
- Tone is educational, not "gotcha." The goal is to be the credible source, not to dunk on other coaching content.

## Footage needed

- Real pitching footage that visually supports the contradiction
- Optional: a simple diagram insert to illustrate the mechanism (AI-generated diagrams are acceptable here)

## Caption rhythm example

```
"THROW HARDER, MOVE FASTER"
NOT ALWAYS TRUE
WATCH THE SEQUENCE HERE
TIMING MOVES THE NEEDLE TOO
WORTH CHECKING YOUR OWN
START YOUR ANALYSIS
```
