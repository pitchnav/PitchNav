# Format D — Product walkthrough

**Best for:** product-explanation videos, website promotional video, paid ads that need to build full understanding.
**Default length:** 45s–60s (see matching timeline files).

## Structure

1. Upload video — real UI, athlete selecting and uploading their side-view clip
2. Enter athlete profile — quick screen recording of the intake form (name, age/level, throwing side)
3. Processing — the processing/progress state, sped up, so the wait doesn't slow the video down
4. Delivery phases — the six-phase screenshot breakdown appearing
5. Mechanics estimates — category scores or observations appearing on screen, clearly labeled as estimates
6. Priority area — the one biggest opportunity/priority, highlighted
7. Drills — the recommended drills list, shown briefly
8. Progress tracking — the rolling training calendar / dashboard view
9. CTA — "Start your Pitch Nav analysis."

## Rules specific to this format

- Every screen must be a real screen recording of the actual product. Do not mock up fake UI.
- Speed-ramp the processing/waiting state rather than cutting it entirely — showing that real analysis happens (not an instant, gimmicky result) supports credibility.
- Keep on-screen labels consistent with actual in-product language (estimates, priorities, drills, progress).
- Do not claim the report includes anything the product doesn't actually deliver.

## Footage needed

- Full screen recording: upload → intake form → processing → report → drills → calendar
- Clean device frame or full-bleed screen capture, consistent across the video
- Optional: brief real pitching footage as a cold open before the walkthrough starts
