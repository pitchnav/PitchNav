# 10-second timeline

Use for: Reel teasers, Story-first cuts, paid-ad bumper variants, and
placements where the platform truncates preview length. Every beat must be
understandable with sound off.

See `_pacing-techniques.md` for the technique menu referenced below.

| Timecode | Beat | Must be true on screen | Suggested technique |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0:00–0:01.5 | Pattern-interrupt hook | The single strongest visual + the hook caption, both in frame immediately | Hard cut on action, caption lands on frame 1 |
| 0:01.5–0:04 | Visual proof + problem, combined | Real pitch footage showing the observation the hook promised | Punch-in zoom or freeze frame on the moment |
| 0:04–0:07 | Insight | One sentence of coaching value — the reason this matters | Diagram insert or joint-marker animation |
| 0:07–0:09 | Product glimpse | Dashboard or report frame, on screen, unmistakably Pitch Nav | UI screen recording, quick |
| 0:09–0:10 | CTA | "Start your Pitch Nav analysis." on screen and (if VO) spoken | Static end card, logo small, bottom-safe |

**Notes:** At 10 seconds there is no room for a second rep or a drill — pick
the single clearest observation. Do not compress the CTA below 1 second; it
must be readable, not just present. Price is almost never included at this
length (see Format G if it must be).
