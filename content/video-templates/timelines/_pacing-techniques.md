# Pacing techniques menu

Every timeline template in this folder references this list. The rule is
simple: **the frame changes every 1-3 seconds, and every change advances the
idea.** A cut, zoom, or caption that doesn't teach something or raise/resolve
tension is a random cut — cut it.

Rotate through these, don't repeat the same one twice in a row:

- Hard cut on action
- Punch-in zoom on the detail that matters
- Slow motion on the mechanical moment being discussed
- Freeze frame, only while a point is actively being explained
- On-screen caption landing on a beat (see `content/captions/caption-style-guide.md`)
- Blue joint-marker animation appearing over the pitcher
- Angle overlay (e.g. a line showing shoulder tilt or hip-shoulder separation)
- Split screen (two reps, or before/after)
- Progress bar / processing animation (product footage only)
- UI screen recording (upload, dashboard, report)
- Speed ramp (full speed into slow motion, or slow motion into full speed)
- Diagram insert (simple line/arrow graphic over footage, electric blue on dark)
- Sound accent on the cut (a single tick, whoosh, or low thud — never a meme sound)

## What NOT to do

- No glitch/VHS/zoom-blur transition packs
- No screen shake for emphasis
- No cutting just to "keep energy up" if nothing new is being shown
- No stacking more than one caption per beat
- No sound effect on every single cut — reserve for the 2-4 biggest beats

## How to read a timeline template

Each duration file gives:

1. A default beat table (start–end, beat name, purpose, suggested technique)
2. What must be true on screen during that beat
3. Notes on what changes if the video is organic vs. a paid ad

Beat boundaries are targets, not laws — hold a shot an extra half-second if
the point needs it. Never hold longer than 3 seconds without a visual change.
