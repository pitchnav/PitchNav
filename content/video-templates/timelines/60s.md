# 60-second timeline

Use for: full product walkthroughs, camera-setup tutorials, and detailed
before-and-after progress videos where the payoff genuinely needs more setup.
This is the longest format in the system — reserve it for content that earns
the extra time, not as a default.

See `_pacing-techniques.md` for the technique menu referenced below.

| Timecode | Beat | Must be true on screen | Suggested technique |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0:00–0:02 | Pattern-interrupt hook | Strongest visual + hook caption | Hard cut on action |
| 0:02–0:07 | Immediate visual proof | Real pitch footage delivering on the hook | Slow motion in |
| 0:07–0:15 | Reveal the problem or surprising observation | The mechanical detail, shown from more than one angle | Freeze frame, angle overlay |
| 0:15–0:25 | Curiosity loop / repeated pattern | Second rep, second angle, or a timeframe comparison | Split screen |
| 0:25–0:38 | Coaching insight or solution | The idea plus a full drill demonstration | Diagram insert, drill B-roll |
| 0:38–0:50 | Product output walkthrough | Full journey: upload, processing, phases, priority, drills, progress tracker | UI screen recording, progress bar |
| 0:50–0:56 | Reinforcement beat | A second proof point (another athlete, another rep, or the before/after side-by-side) — unique to this length | Split screen or quick montage |
| 0:56–1:00 | CTA | "Start your Pitch Nav analysis." Price only if the format calls for it | Static end card |

**Notes:** At 60 seconds the temptation is to slow down — resist it. The
1-3 second visual-change rule still applies for the entire runtime. If a beat
can't sustain a change every 1-3 seconds, the content for that beat is too
thin and should be cut rather than stretched.
