# Pitching footage checklist

Print this or open it on a phone during the shoot. Camera setup must match
`content/video-templates/formats/format-c-camera-mistake.md` exactly for any
clip that will be used for a real mechanics observation.

## Camera setup (verify before every athlete)

- [ ] True side view, from the throwing-arm side
- [ ] Full body visible in frame, top to bottom, at every phase of the delivery
- [ ] Camera approximately 15 feet from the pitcher
- [ ] Phone lens approximately 6 feet high, level
- [ ] Landscape orientation
- [ ] Stationary camera — tripod or fixed surface, no handheld
- [ ] No digital zoom
- [ ] Strong, even lighting, no long shadows across the mound
- [ ] SLO-MO recording mode selected (not normal video, not slowed down after)
- [ ] 240 FPS if available, 120 FPS minimum acceptable

## Footage to capture per athlete

- [ ] Full-speed side view, 240 FPS side view (multiple reps)
- [ ] 120 FPS backup (in case 240 FPS has an issue)
- [ ] Rear-view option, if a second camera is available (not required)
- [ ] Close-up: glove
- [ ] Close-up: baseball grip
- [ ] Close-up: cleats on mound / rubber
- [ ] Close-up: landing-foot close-up at foot strike
- [ ] Full delivery, wide, multiple reps
- [ ] Repeated pitch — at least 2 reps of the same pitch type back to back,
      matched framing, for split-screen/pattern-confirmation edits
- [ ] Drill demonstration, if a specific coaching cue will be shown

## After the shoot

- [ ] Confirm at least one full rep plays back clearly in slow motion before
      wrapping (check on a laptop, not just the phone screen)
- [ ] Get written consent to use the athlete's footage and (if applicable)
      first name/likeness in marketing
- [ ] Back up footage before leaving the field
